Wrath of Air vs Windfury vs Superior Wizard Oil

DPS & Threat contribution for a TBC Prot Paladin tank (patch 2.4.3) — companion to prot_paladin_rotations.html

Question being answered

If a shaman in your party can drop one Air totem (only one Air totem can be active at a time per shaman), and you can optionally weapon-coat with Superior Wizard Oil, which adds the most DPS and TPS to your rotation? Wrath of Air Totem (WoA) and Windfury Totem (WF) compete for the same Air slot. Wizard Oil is independent of either.

Baseline Assumptions (Same as Main Rotation Guide)

T4/T5 raid-geared profile, raid-buffed (Flask only — Wizard Oil not assumed): 709 spell power • 436 AP • Fang of the Leviathan (1.80 speed, 41.1 weapon DPS, +40 spell damage) • ~9.5% melee crit / ~11% spell crit • 2/2 Improved Holy Shield (8 charges, +20% HS damage) • 1.9× Righteous Fury • 4/5 Justicar — 2pc (×1.10 on seal procs / SoC DoT) + 4pc (+15 flat per HS block) • Seal of Righteousness as primary seal • Level 73 boss, ~7,700 armor.

Hit gaps: melee miss 6%, spell miss 11.89%. Both Air totems and Wizard Oil are self/party buffs — not raid-wide: only your shaman's 5-man party gets the buff. This guide assumes your Prot Paladin is in that party.

Totem Twisting: What If The Shaman Cycles Both?

A shaman with one Air totem slot can twist — alternate dropping WF then WoA on a cycle — so the party gets partial uptime on each. The question: does twisting give the Prot Paladin more threat than just running one totem full-time?

Critical TBC mechanic: in TBC 2.4.3, Windfury Totem is a continuous aura, not a pulse-and-persist buff like vanilla. When you replace WF with WoA, the WF proc effect ends immediately — there is no carry-over window where both are active. Twisting gives strict alternating uptime, not overlap. (This is the opposite of WotLK, where WF Totem's buff lasted ~10s after dropping — that's where the famous twisting strats come from.)

Modeling the Twist (10s / 10s cycle)

Realistic twist: drop WF, leave it up for ~10 seconds, swap to WoA for ~10 seconds, repeat. Faster (5s) twisting captures more WF procs but burns extra shaman mana on totem casts and GCDs. The 10s cycle gives 50% uptime each.

Expected gains per 60s twisted cycle (Imp WF + base WoA from Enhancement shaman): WoA uptime: 30s × 65 TPS (base WoA on holy abilities) = 1,950 threat WF uptime: 30s × 0.111 procs/s × 582 threat/proc = 1,940 threat Total twisted (60s) = ~3,890 threat For comparison (same 60s window): Imp WoA full uptime 78 × 60 = 4,680 threat Imp WF full uptime 6.67 procs × 582 = 3,880 threat Base WoA full uptime 65 × 60 = 3,900 threat Base WF full uptime 6.67 procs × ~520 = 3,470 threat

The Surprising Result

Strategy 60s threat vs Imp WoA alone vs Imp WF alone
Imp WoA full uptime (Resto/Ele shaman) 4,680 — +800 (+21%)
Base WoA + Imp WF twisting (Enh shaman) ~3,890 −790 (−17%) +10 (basically equal)
Imp WF full uptime (Enh shaman) ~3,880 −800 (−17%) —
Imp WoA + base WF twisting (Resto shaman) ~4,070 −610 (−13%) +190 (+5%)
Base WoA full uptime (Resto/Ele shaman) ~3,900 −780 (−17%) +20
For the Prot Paladin's threat specifically, twisting is a wash or a slight loss. The Imp version of either totem at 100% uptime beats any twisting combo, because losing half the uptime of an Imp buff costs more than gaining half the uptime of the un-Imp'd one.

So why do shamans twist at all? Because the totem affects the whole party:
  • During WF time: melee DPS in the party get extra attacks too — their gain is much bigger than yours.
  • During WoA time: any caster in the party (healing paladin, mage, ele shaman, lock) gets +101 SP — raid-DPS gain on top of your threat.
  • Net party output goes up; your individual TPS stays flat or drops slightly.
Bottom line for you, the tank: you have no reason to ask a shaman to twist. If they do it for the party's sake, expect your threat-add to land around ~65 TPS — basically identical to keeping one totem up the whole time.
Caveats on the twist model:
  • Shaman pays ~190 mana per totem drop + 1.0s GCD. At a 10s cycle = 12 drops/min = 2,280 mana/min, more than most Resto shamans can sustain alongside healing.
  • Each totem drop creates a small dead-zone window (one GCD) where neither buff is up. The 50/50 model above ignores this; in practice uptime drops to ~45/45 with a 10s cycle.
  • WF procs are random. The "0.111 procs/s" rate is expected value — in any single 30s window you might get 2 or 5 procs. WoA's scaling is deterministic.
  • If your shaman is in a different 5-man party (e.g. healer group), you get neither totem. The values above all assume the shaman is in your party.

Verdict & Practical Decision Tree

Wizard Oil: always yes. It's a self-applied weapon coat with no party-comp requirement. ~21 TPS for the cost of one consumable per hour. Only skip it if you're main-sealing Seal of Blood, in which case Superior Mana Oil (+14 MP5) is the better trade.
Air totem: it's close, but lean WoA in raids.
  • Imp WF (~65 TPS) ties Imp WoA (~78 TPS at threat, but only ~32 DPS). For raw threat per second, Imp WoA edges out Imp WF in the SoR rotation.
  • WF is strictly party-local melee. WoA's value is shared by other casters in the party (healing paladin, ele shaman, mage in melee group, etc.), so it has higher raid utility.
  • WF's threat is bursty. A 9s gap with no proc, then a 582-threat spike. Bad for taunt-races where TPS averages don't matter, only the floor.
  • If you swap to Seal of Blood (Horde, on physical bosses where SoB outdamages SoR), WF flips ahead: WoA loses scaling on the SoB proc itself (0 SP coef), while WF still gives the extra swing.
Default party setup for a Prot Paladin:
  1. 1 Enhancement shaman in the melee party with Windfury Totem + Strength of Earth — Imp WF benefits the whole melee group, you piggyback for ~65 TPS.
  2. If no Enhancement is available, request Wrath of Air Totem from any Elemental/Resto shaman — ~78 TPS with Imp WoA, only slightly behind Imp WF, and helps any caster in your party too.
  3. Either way, always coat with Superior Wizard Oil for the +21 TPS on top of whichever totem you get.
Caveats and corner cases:
  • Threat ≠ DPS. WoA's TPS is inflated by Righteous Fury (1.9×) because every gain is Holy. WF's TPS is partially physical (1.0× RF). Compare DPS columns for a "fairness" check — there WoA and WF are nearly identical.
  • SoR-cap weapons. SoR damage caps at a per-swing amount based on weapon speed. With a slow 2H, SoR procs are bigger but rarer; with a 1.8s 1H, they're smaller but more frequent. Numbers above use the rotation guide's 1.8s spell-power weapon.
  • JotC debuff. Not modeled in these numbers. If your raid is running a JotC rotation (Judgement of the Crusader = +219 Holy damage taken), all WoA and Wizard Oil scaling is also amplified by that debuff. WF's physical portion is not.
  • Mana cost. None of these three buffs costs you any mana. WoA does cost the shaman a totem slot, WF does too. Wizard Oil is gold (~3g per stack of 5).
  • Hit and miss. Spell miss 11.89% applies to JoR/Exo/HoW/AS. Numbers above are post-miss expected values. WF extra swings still roll against the boss's miss table (6% white miss after hit).

One-Line Bottom Line

Raid rotation with SoR: Imp WoA ≈ Imp WF ≈ +65–78 TPS, Wizard Oil ≈ +21 TPS.
Bring all you can. Wizard Oil + whichever Air totem your shaman drops — that's your full threat-amp from this category.

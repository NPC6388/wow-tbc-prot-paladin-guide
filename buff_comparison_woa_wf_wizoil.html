Wrath of Air vs Windfury vs Superior Wizard Oil

DPS & Threat contribution for a TBC Prot Paladin tank (patch 2.4.3) — companion to prot_paladin_rotations.html

Question being answered

If a shaman in your party can drop one Air totem (only one Air totem can be active at a time per shaman), and you can optionally weapon-coat with Superior Wizard Oil, which combination adds the most DPS and TPS to your rotation? Two slot conflicts drive the answer: (1) Wrath of Air Totem (WoA) and Windfury Totem (WF) compete for the shaman's Air totem slot, and — the one people miss — (2) Windfury Totem and Superior Wizard Oil compete for your weapon's temporary-enhancement slot.

The mechanic that decides this (TBC 2.4.3): Windfury Totem does not give you a stat aura — it applies a temporary weapon enhancement to every party member (the “Windfury” weapon buff that drives the extra-attack procs). That enhancement lives in the same single weapon-enchant slot as Superior Wizard Oil, sharpening stones, weightstones and Mana Oil — so Windfury Totem and Wizard Oil cannot coexist on your weapon — applying Wizard Oil overwrites the Windfury buff (and the totem won’t re-stamp over your oil), so you run one or the other. This is the Classic/Vanilla behavior, and it persists through all of TBC — Windfury Totem was only reworked into a pure stat aura in Wrath of the Lich King. By contrast, Wrath of Air Totem is a stat aura (+spell power), not a weapon enhancement, so it stacks freely with Wizard Oil. That single difference, more than any per-second number below, is what separates the two Air totems for you.

Baseline Assumptions (Same as Main Rotation Guide)

T4/T5 raid-geared profile, raid-buffed (Flask only — Wizard Oil not assumed): 709 spell power • 436 AP • Fang of the Leviathan (1.80 speed, 41.1 weapon DPS, +40 spell damage) • ~9.5% melee crit / ~11% spell crit • 2/2 Improved Holy Shield (8 charges, +20% HS damage) • 1.9× Righteous Fury • 4/5 Justicar — 2pc (×1.10 on seal procs / SoC DoT) + 4pc (+15 flat per HS block) • Seal of Righteousness as primary seal • Level 73 boss, ~7,700 armor.

Hit gaps: melee miss 6%, spell miss 11.89%. Both Air totems and Wizard Oil are self/party buffs — not raid-wide: only your shaman's 5-man party gets the buff. This guide assumes your Prot Paladin is in that party.

Combining Buffs: The Real Choice Is “WoA + Oil” vs “Windfury Alone”

Because Windfury Totem and Wizard Oil fight over the same weapon slot, you can never actually run all three at once. Your realistic loadouts collapse to just two packages — and that is what you should actually be comparing:

Package What you get Combined SP to Holy threat Steady TPS from this category
WoA + Oil (stacks) Imp WoA stat aura and Wizard Oil on your weapon — both apply at once 121 + 42 = 163 SP ~78 + ~21 = ~99 TPS
WoA + Oil (base WoA) Non-Imp WoA + Wizard Oil 101 + 42 = 143 SP ~65 + ~21 = ~86 TPS
WoA + WF via totem twisting (no Oil) A dedicated twisting shaman keeps both air-totem effects up at once — the WF weapon buff persists through the swap (TBC mechanic). Still no oil. 101 SP (base WoA) + WF procs ~122 TPS — best case (see Twisting)
WF alone (no Oil) Imp Windfury Totem — to keep its weapon buff you must run no oil, since applying Wizard Oil would wipe it 0 SP (WF grants none) ~65 TPS
WF + Oil Not possible — the two share your weapon slot. Apply Wizard Oil and it wipes the Windfury buff; to keep Windfury you leave your weapon un-oiled. You get one or the other, never the sum.
Net result: the honest comparison isn’t “WoA ~78 vs WF ~65.” It’s WoA + Oil ≈ ~99 TPS vs WF ≈ ~65 TPS, because choosing Windfury costs you the ~21 TPS of Wizard Oil you’d otherwise keep. That turns a slim totem-vs-totem edge into a ~34 TPS advantage for the WoA package in the SoR rotation. WF only claws it back when its melee value to you plus its value to the rest of your melee party outweighs that gap — a party-DPS argument, not a personal-threat one. The only thing that beats WoA + Oil for you is totem twisting (~122 TPS), which stacks both air-totem effects — see the Totem Twisting section below.
Practical consequence for the tank: if your party’s shaman runs Windfury Totem, don’t coat with Wizard Oil at all — every coat just wipes the Windfury buff off your weapon. Oil up only when your Air totem is Wrath of Air (or when no shaman in your party is dropping Windfury). Choosing to keep your oil means giving up the Windfury buff entirely.

Totem Twisting: What If The Shaman Cycles Both?

A shaman with one Air totem slot can twist — keep WoA down as the resident totem, then briefly re-drop Windfury every few seconds to refresh its weapon buff before it expires, then swap straight back to WoA. The question: does twisting give the Prot Paladin more threat than just running one totem full-time? In TBC, the answer is yes — a lot more.

Critical TBC mechanic: in TBC, Windfury Totem applies a temporary weapon buff that persists for several seconds after the totem itself is replaced — the same pulse-and-persist behavior as vanilla. That carry-over window is exactly what makes twisting work: drop WF, swap the Air slot back to WoA, and for the lingering window the party benefits from both the persisting WF weapon buff and the live WoA aura at once. Done well, you approach full uptime on both totems' effects simultaneously. (This only changed in WotLK patch 3.0, when WF Totem became a continuous aura that ends the instant it's replaced — which is what killed twisting. It was alive and well all through TBC.)

Modeling the Twist

Because the WF weapon buff carries over, WoA stays the “resident” totem and is only interrupted for the brief GCD it takes to re-stamp Windfury. So WoA runs at ~88% uptime while the WF buff stays refreshed at ~100%. The two stack on you.

Expected gains per 60s twist (Enhancement shaman: Imp WF + base WoA): WF weapon buff ~100% × 65 TPS (Imp WF procs + their SoR procs) = ~65 TPS WoA aura ~88% × 65 TPS (base WoA — Enh has no Imp WoA) = ~57 TPS Wizard Oil 0 (run un-oiled; a coat would wipe the WF buff) = 0 TPS Combined twist = ~122 TPS (~7,300 / 60s) For comparison (same 60s window, best realistic non-twist packages): Imp WoA + Wizard Oil 99 × 60 = 5,940 (Ele/Resto shaman + your oil, they stack) Base WoA + Wizard Oil 86 × 60 = 5,160 Imp WF alone (no oil) 65 × 60 = 3,900 (run un-oiled; oil wipes WF) Imp WoA alone (no oil) 78 × 60 = 4,680

For the Prot Paladin’s threat, a real twist is the best case — if you can get it. Because the Windfury weapon buff persists through the totem swap, a dedicated twisting shaman gives you WoA and WF at the same time (~122 TPS), clearly ahead of any single-totem package. The catch is cost — twisting is essentially all the shaman does:
  • It eats ~190 mana per drop and near-constant GCDs — a Resto shaman who’s also healing can’t sustain it. Realistically only an Enhancement shaman (already meleeing in your group) twists.
  • An Enhancement twister has Imp WF but not Imp WoA, so your WoA portion is the base 101 SP value, not 121.
  • You forfeit Wizard Oil regardless — any coat you apply would wipe the WF buff, so you run un-oiled all fight.
Bottom line for you, the tank: if a shaman is already twisting WF/WoA for the melee group, you’re the incidental big winner (~122 TPS). Don’t demand a twist purely for your own threat — it’s a heavy ask. Without one, WoA + Wizard Oil (~99 TPS) is your reliable best.
Caveats on the twist model:
  • The ~122 TPS figure is an idealized, well-executed twist. A sloppy or part-time twist lands somewhere between this and single-totem values.
  • Twisting relies on the WF weapon buff’s carry-over lasting long enough to cover each WoA window. If the shaman lets it lapse, WF uptime drops and the procs go with it.
  • WF procs are random — the 0.111 procs/s rate is an expected value; any given 10s window might land 0 or 2.
  • Twisting forfeits your Wizard Oil entirely — you must run un-oiled so the WF buff stays up (a coat would wipe it). That cost is already baked into the ~122 TPS above.
  • If your shaman is in a different 5-man party (e.g. healer group), you get neither totem. Everything here assumes the shaman is in your party.

Verdict & Practical Decision Tree

Wizard Oil: yes — unless your party runs Windfury Totem. It’s a self-applied weapon coat worth ~21 TPS for one consumable per hour, and it stacks on top of Wrath of Air. But it shares your weapon slot with the Windfury Totem buff, so in a Windfury party it’s oil or the totem, not both — and the totem (~65 TPS) wins, so you skip the oil there. (Don’t reach for Superior Mana Oil instead — even when your seal doesn’t scale with spell power, everything else in your kit does, so Wizard Oil is always the better weapon coat for a threat tank.)
Air totem: lean WoA — and it’s not as close as the raw totem numbers suggest.
  • Totem-vs-totem, Imp WoA (~78 TPS) already edges Imp WF (~65 TPS) in the SoR rotation. But the real gap is bigger: WoA lets you keep your Wizard Oil (+21 TPS), while a Windfury party means running un-oiled (a coat wipes the WF buff). So the package comparison is ~99 TPS (WoA + Oil) vs ~65 TPS (WF, no oil) — about a ~34 TPS swing.
  • WF is strictly party-local melee. WoA's value is shared by other casters in the party (healing paladin, ele shaman, mage in melee group, etc.), so it has higher raid utility.
  • WF's threat is bursty. A 9s gap with no proc, then a 582-threat spike. Bad for taunt-races where TPS averages don't matter, only the floor.
  • Seal of Blood doesn’t change this. SoB is only used to land a single big Judgement of Blood on the pull (the highest-threat opener) — you’re back on Seal of Righteousness for the entire sustained fight. So the comparison always runs under SoR, where WoA + Oil leads. (Even the on-pull JoB carries a 0.43 spell-power coefficient, so WoA and Wizard Oil still feed it.)
Default party setup for a Prot Paladin:
  1. For your personal threat, Wrath of Air is the pick — ~78 TPS from Imp WoA plus the ~21 TPS from the Wizard Oil it lets you keep = ~99 TPS. Request WoA from any Elemental/Resto shaman in your party and coat with Superior Wizard Oil.
  2. If a Windfury party is better for the raid (an Enhancement shaman dropping Imp WF for a stacked melee group), you piggyback for ~65 TPS — but go un-oiled, since any coat you apply just wipes the WF buff. Don’t waste oil charges in a Windfury party.
  3. Wrath of Air also helps every caster in your party (healing paladin, ele shaman, mage), so it usually wins on raid utility too — unless the melee stack makes Windfury the group’s clear choice.
Caveats and corner cases:
  • Threat ≠ DPS. WoA's TPS is inflated by Righteous Fury (1.9×) because every gain is Holy. WF's TPS is partially physical (1.0× RF). Compare DPS columns for a "fairness" check — there WoA and WF are nearly identical.
  • SoR-cap weapons. SoR damage caps at a per-swing amount based on weapon speed. With a slow 2H, SoR procs are bigger but rarer; with a 1.8s 1H, they're smaller but more frequent. Numbers above use the rotation guide's 1.8s spell-power weapon.
  • JotC debuff. Not modeled in these numbers. If your raid is running a JotC rotation (Judgement of the Crusader = +219 Holy damage taken), all WoA and Wizard Oil scaling is also amplified by that debuff. WF's physical portion is not.
  • Mana cost. None of these three buffs costs you any mana. WoA does cost the shaman a totem slot, WF does too. Wizard Oil is gold (~3g per stack of 5).
  • Hit and miss. Spell miss 11.89% applies to JoR/Exo/HoW/AS. Numbers above are post-miss expected values. WF extra swings still roll against the boss's miss table (6% white miss after hit).

One-Line Bottom Line

Raid rotation with SoR: WoA + Wizard Oil ≈ +99 TPS (they stack) beats Windfury alone ≈ +65 TPS (run un-oiled).
You can’t bring all three. Windfury Totem and Wizard Oil share your weapon slot — so it’s Wrath of Air + Wizard Oil for max personal threat, or Windfury (no oil) if the raid needs it for the melee group. The one exception: a shaman twisting WF/WoA stacks both air effects on you for ~122 TPS — the true ceiling, but a heavy ask.

Map & Route Through Karazhan

Karazhan is a vertical tower. You enter at the bottom and work your way up. The standard route is shown below.

Standard Clear Order

1 Attumen the Huntsman (Stables, ground floor) → 2 Moroes (Banquet Hall) → 3 Maiden of Virtue (Guest Chambers, side wing) → 4 Opera Event (Opera Hall, back entrance) → 5 Nightbane (Master's Terrace, if attuned - optional) → 6 The Curator (Menagerie, up the Broken Stair) → 7 Shade of Aran (Guardian's Library) → 8 Terestian Illhoof (off the Library hallway) → 9 Netherspite (side room past Library) → 10 Chess Event (Gamesman's Hall) → 11 Prince Malchezaar (Netherspace, top of tower)

Tower Layout (Floor-by-Floor)

Navigation Tips:
  • After killing Opera, go backstage and up to reach the Broken Stair leading to Curator.
  • The Master's Terrace (Nightbane) is accessed through the backstage area after Opera.
  • After Curator, continue up through the library halls to reach Shade, then Illhoof and Netherspite are in side rooms off the same hallway.
  • After Chess, there's a short spiral stair up to Prince's platform in the Netherspace.
  • The Servants' Quarters (animal bosses) is a side area accessible from the entry — you can skip it or clear it first.

Key Shortcuts

3 Shortcuts You Must Know:
  1. Berthold's Teleport (most important): After killing Shade of Aran, speak to Berthold the Doorman at the main entrance to teleport directly to the Guardian's Library. Skips the entire lower instance on wipe recovery.
  2. Opera Side Entrance (external): After the Opera Event is cleared, an external side door of the tower unlocks. From outside, go right from the main entrance, cross the small creek, up stairs to a bridge, and through the door. Zones you in at the Opera level.
  3. Servants' Entrance Door: In the Broken Stair area, a locked door can be opened from the upper side. Once opened, it connects down to the Ballroom mezzanine, letting you skip Opera on subsequent trips up.

General Prot Paladin Tips for Karazhan

Karazhan is where Prot Paladins shine. The large trash packs, undead mobs, and multi-add boss fights play directly to your strengths. Consecration + Holy Shield + Retribution Aura makes you the AoE king.

Blessing of Sacrifice — Your Secret Weapon

BoSac (30-sec CD) transfers damage to you from the target. Since taking damage breaks most CC effects, this lets you break yourself out of:

  • Gouge (Moroes) — BoSac on OT/melee, Moroes hits them, damage transfers to you, Gouge breaks instantly
  • Repentance (Maiden) — BoSac on someone in Holy Ground range
  • Fear (Nightbane, Big Bad Wolf, Dorothee) — BoSac on anyone taking damage
  • Polymorph (Shade of Aran mass poly) — BoSac on someone taking Blizzard/DoT damage

This is the single most powerful Prot Paladin utility in Karazhan. Use it proactively before CC mechanics hit.

Holy Shield & Uncrushable

102.4% combined avoidance + block with Holy Shield active = uncrushable. This is mandatory for all raid bosses.

  • Holy Shield lasts 10 sec on an 8-sec cooldown — 2-sec overlap window. Never let it drop.
  • Prioritize refreshing Holy Shield above Consecration and Judgment on boss fights.
  • Do NOT cast Avenger's Shield mid-fight on bosses — the GCD can cause Holy Shield to lapse, opening a crush window.
  • Defense cap: 490 for crit immunity.
Key Consumables: Ironshield Potion (boss fights), Super Mana Potion (long fights), Flask of Blinding Light (threat) or Flask of Fortification (survival), Elixir of Major Defense + Elixir of Draenic Wisdom (if not flasking). Flame Cap for burst threat on pull. Nightmare Seed for emergency HP (Prince Phase 2).
Prot Paladin Weaknesses to Watch: No interrupt (bring other interrupters for Shade, Moroes adds, etc.). No spell reflect. Single-target threat ramp-up can be slower than Warriors on pull — use Avenger's Shield + Judgment of Blood for snap threat. Fear is your enemy (no Berserker Rage) — coordinate Fear Ward, Tremor Totem, and BoSac for Nightbane/Moroes. Silences shut down your entire kit (Shadikith, Wanton Hostess wraith form). Spectral Retainer Antimagic Pulse strips Righteous Fury — keep RF on your bar.

1. Attumen the Huntsman

Stables Trash

The first trash in Karazhan. Mostly undead horses and their handlers. Great for Prot Paladin AoE.

Spectral Stallion

  • Stomping — AoE knockback to nearby players, minor physical damage
  • Absorb Vitality — Drains health from the target, healing itself
  • Non-elite, pulled in packs. Consecrate and tank multiples easily.

Spectral Charger

  • Charge — Charges a random distant player, dealing physical damage and stunning briefly
  • Absorb Vitality — Drains health, healing the mob
  • Will charge ranged players — warn your group to not stand too far back.

Spectral Stable Hand

  • Healing Touch — Nature. Heals nearby horses for a significant amount. Interrupt with Hammer of Justice if no other interrupt!
  • Knockdown — 2-sec stun on tank
  • Pierce Armor — Stacking armor reduction debuff. Dangerous when tanking multiple mobs.
  • Whip Frenzy — Buffs nearby Chargers/Stallions, increasing their attack speed
  • Kill Priority: Kill or CC these first to prevent heals and Pierce Armor stacks.
Undead mobs! Use Exorcism on pull for snap threat, and use Holy Wrath as an AoE stun + damage on big packs. This is where you dominate.

ATTUMEN THE HUNTSMAN & MIDNIGHT

Overview

Two-phase fight. You engage Midnight (the horse) first. At 95% HP, Attumen spawns. At 25% Midnight HP, Attumen mounts up and the fight becomes single target.

Phase 1: Midnight (100% → 25%)

  • Knockdown — Physical. Stuns the tank for 3 sec. Unavoidable.
  • Summon Attumen — At 95% Midnight HP, Attumen spawns and must be picked up by an OT or the MT grabs both.

Attumen (while dismounted)

  • Shadow Cleave — Shadow. Frontal cleave dealing ~4,000-6,000 shadow damage. Face away from raid!
  • Intangible Presence — Shadow. Curse, reduces chance to hit by 50% on nearby targets. Can be decursed.
  • Berserker Charge — Charges a random player, dealing damage and briefly stunning them.

Phase 2: Mounted Attumen (Midnight at 25%)

  • Attumen mounts Midnight — they become one mob. Full HP reset to 100%. THREAT TABLE WIPES.
  • Retains Shadow Cleave, Intangible Presence, and Berserker Charge.
  • Berserker Charge has a minimum 8-yard range — if the entire raid stacks within 8 yards, he can't charge anyone.
  • Knockdown — Inherited from Midnight. Still stuns the tank.
  • Hits harder in mounted form. Use cooldowns if needed.
Tank Midnight and let the OT grab Attumen when he spawns (or solo-tank both if geared). Face Attumen away from the raid due to Shadow Cleave. Consecrate hits both when they're near each other. After mount-up, straightforward tank and spank.
Intangible Presence (-50% hit) must be decursed ASAP, especially on the tank. Without decurse it massively reduces your threat output.

2. Moroes

Banquet Hall Trash

The banquet hall is filled with undead ghosts at dining tables. Many packs of 4-6 mobs. Another area where Prot Paladin AoE is extremely strong.

Ghostly Steward

  • Drunken Skull Crack — Physical. 4-second stun on highest threat target
  • Enrage — At 50% HP, enrages and wipes threat, increases damage significantly
  • Immune to Shackle, Taunt, and Stun. Both tanks should build threat before DPS. Re-establish threat after Enrage.

Phantom Guest

  • Searing Pain — Fire nuke. Caster mob.
  • Immolate — Fire DoT
  • Curse of Agony — Shadow DoT
  • Some variants heal. Interrupt or CC caster variants. Can be shackled.

Ghostly Philanthropist

  • Incite Rage — Buffs nearby ghosts, increasing their damage
  • Low damage itself but empowers the pack. Kill early.

Phantom Valet

  • Demoralizing Shout — AoE attack power reduction
  • Kick — Interrupt + silence
  • Annoying but low priority. Can be shackled.

Skeletal Waiter

  • Brittle Bones — Curse. Increases ALL damage taken by 35% for 10 seconds. DECURSE IMMEDIATELY!
  • Undead. Use Exorcism/Holy Wrath. Shackle or AoE down. Kill or CC these to prevent Brittle Bones on the tank.

Spectral Retainer (DANGER)

  • Oath of Fealty — Mind controls a raid member. Cannot be dispelled (unlike Priest MC). Sheep the MC'd player.
  • Antimagic Pulse — AoE mass dispel that strips ALL buffs from nearby players, INCLUDING RIGHTEOUS FURY! If RF is removed, you lose massive threat instantly.
  • Rend — Physical bleed on highest threat target
  • Immune to Shackle, Taunt, and Stun. Use Grounding Totem if available. FIRST KILL PRIORITY in every pack. Re-apply Righteous Fury immediately if dispelled.
All undead! This is Prot Paladin paradise. Holy Wrath stuns the entire pack. Consecrate holds everything. Use Exorcism on pull for snap threat. EXCEPT: Watch for Spectral Retainers! Their Antimagic Pulse strips Righteous Fury. Always kill Retainers first and keep RF on your action bar for instant reapplication.

MOROES

Overview

Moroes spawns with 4 random dinner guests (adds). The adds must be CC'd or killed. Moroes applies a lethal bleed (Garrote) throughout the fight.

Moroes' Abilities

  • Vanish — Moroes vanishes and reappears, applying Garrote to a random player. Resets threat temporarily.
  • Garrote — Physical bleed. Deals ~1,000 damage every 3 seconds for 5 minutes. Persists through death! Only removed by: Blessing of Protection, Ice Block, Divine Shield, Stoneform, or killing Moroes.
  • Gouge — Incapacitates the current tank for 6 seconds. OT must immediately pick up Moroes. Facing away prevents it (but risky).
  • Blind — 10-second disorient on a nearby melee player.
  • Enrage — At 30% HP, increases damage dealt by 10% and attack speed.

Strategy: Shackle 2 adds, trap 1, OT or kill the 4th. As MT, focus Moroes. Use Blessing of Protection on Garroted players who are dying (removes the bleed!). This is one of your unique contributions. You can also use Turn Evil as emergency CC on one undead add.

Gouge Trick (Pally exclusive): Cast Blessing of Sacrifice on the OT or a melee DPS before Gouge is expected. When Moroes Gouges you, the incoming damage to the BoSac target transfers damage back to you, which immediately breaks the Gouge. Moroes stays on you with zero downtime. This is one of the strongest Prot Paladin advantages in all of Karazhan.
Gouge (without BoSac trick): Moroes will Gouge the tank regularly. You MUST have an OT ready to taunt immediately. Communicate with your OT. After Gouge wears off, taunt back.

3. Maiden of Virtue

Guest Chambers Trash

Undead maids and ghostly concubines in the side hallway leading to Maiden's room.

Night Mistress / Night Lord

  • Shadow Bolt — Shadow. 2,200-2,800 damage nuke
  • Impending Betrayal — Shadow. At 50% HP, transforms to ghost form and applies this debuff: ~3,000 shadow damage to the raid after 10 sec
  • Spread out to avoid splash. Can be shackled. Warn healers about the 50% HP phase change burst.

Wanton Hostess / Wanton Host

  • Alluring Aura (above 50% HP) — Reduces physical damage dealt by 50% for nearby players
  • Bewitching Aura (below 50% HP) — Reduces spell damage dealt by 50% for nearby players
  • Banshee Shriek — 5-sec silence to nearby enemies
  • At 50% transforms to Wraith form, breaking all CC. Can be shackled before transformation.

Phantom Guardsman

  • Consecrate — Holy. AoE damage zone — move out
  • Knockdown — Stun for several seconds
  • Shield Slam — High-damage physical hit
  • Summon Phantom Hound — Summons an add on pull
  • Hits for 5,000-7,000. Can be shackled.

Spectral Sentry

  • Shoot — Ranged physical attack on highest aggro
  • Multi-Shot — Hits 3 random players for 3,150-3,850 physical damage
  • Stationary ranged mob. LoS pull to you. Multi-Shot hurts clothies. Can be shackled.

Phantom Attendant

  • Kick — Interrupt + 4 second silence
  • Shadow Rejuvenation — Shadow. HoT healing ~2,000/sec on allies. Dispel or kill fast.
  • Melee mob that heals. Can be shackled.
Undead — Exorcism and Holy Wrath work here. Wanton Hostess Alluring Aura does NOT affect Holy damage — your Consecration, Holy Shield, and Judgment are unaffected by the 50% physical damage reduction, making Prot Pally actually strong against these. Watch for Banshee Shriek silence from the Wraith form though.

MAIDEN OF VIRTUE

Overview

Single-target fight in a circular room. Deals primarily Holy damage which bypasses your armor. This is one of the harder fights for Prot Paladins because your mitigation is less effective.

Abilities

  • Repentance — Holy. AoE 12-second incapacitate on the entire raid every ~30-35 seconds, dealing 1,750-2,250 holy damage. Breaks on subsequent damage. She continues meleeing the tank during Repentance. Healers must be topped before this hits.
  • Holy Fire — Holy. DoT on a random player. Deals 3,238-3,762 initial damage + 1,750 every 2 seconds for 12 seconds. Very heavy damage.
  • Holy Ground — Holy. Persistent AoE aura dealing 240-360 Holy damage every 3 seconds and silencing anyone within ~12 yards for 1 second. Melee must eat this. Ranged should stay out.
  • Holy Wrath — Holy. Chain damage that bounces between nearby players, dealing increasing damage per bounce. Spread out to limit bounces.
Actually better than you'd think for Prot Pally. Holy Ground's silence only lasts 1 second per 3-second tick — since all Prot Paladin abilities are instant cast, you can fit them between silence ticks without issue. Your threat generation is barely affected. Use Ironshield Potion (she still melees). Keep Holy Shield up for block value mitigation on her melee swings. During Repentance, you'll take unhealed melee hits for up to 12 sec — use Ardent Defender passive. BoSac trick: Cast Blessing of Sacrifice on someone taking Holy Ground damage — the transferred damage will break your Repentance early!
Repentance timing: Holy Ground ticks will break Repentance on melee after a few seconds, but healers at range stay stunned the full duration. The tank takes unhealed damage during this window. Make sure you're near full HP when Repentance hits.
Positioning: Tank Maiden in the center. Ranged and healers spread out around the edges of the room, staying out of Holy Ground range. Melee stack behind her.

4. Opera Event

Opera Hall Trash

The path to Opera goes through the Grand Ballroom with ghostly guests and then through the audience hall with skeletal ushers.

Spectral Performer

  • Spotlight — Holy. AoE damage around the mob, deals fire/holy damage in a radius
  • Curtain Call — Disorient effect on nearby players
  • Ranged caster. Pull with Avenger's Shield. Can be CC'd.

Skeletal Usher (DANGER)

  • Ice Tomb — Frost. Encases the highest-threat target in ice for 10 seconds. WIPES THE THREAT TABLE. All mobs go loose after this. Must DPS the ice block to free the player.
  • Frost Shock — Frost. 2,800-3,500 damage nuke + slow
  • Magnetic Pull — Pulls a random distant player to the Usher
  • Undead. Shackle the extras. OT must be ready to pick up all mobs when the MT gets Ice Tombed. Grounding Totem can absorb Ice Tomb. Free Action Potion prevents the freeze.

Ghostly Philanthropist (also found here)

  • Incite Rage — Buffs nearby allies' damage
  • Kill early to prevent buffing the pack.

Spectral Patron

  • Kick — Interrupt + silence
  • Goblet of Flames — Fire. Throws a burning goblet at a player, dealing AoE fire damage on impact
  • Melee mob. Straightforward to tank.
Mostly undead again. The Skeletal Ushers are the most dangerous trash here — Ice Tomb can kill if not broken fast, and Frost Shock hurts. Shackle extras and focus them down. Consecrate holds the rest easily.

OPERA: THE BIG BAD WOLF

Overview

Single-target boss. Periodically transforms a random player into Little Red Riding Hood who must kite the boss.

Abilities

  • Little Red Riding Hood — Transforms a random raid member into Red Riding Hood for 15 seconds. They move faster but cannot use abilities and have reduced armor. The Wolf chases them — they must kite! If the Wolf reaches them, he one-shots them.
  • Terrifying Howl — Physical. AoE fear for 3 seconds on nearby players.
  • Wide Swipe — Frontal cone knockback.
Tank and spank when you're not Red Riding Hood. If you get transformed, run! Kite around the stage edges. When someone else is Red Riding Hood, the Wolf ignores threat and chases them — just keep DPSing until he returns. Blessing of Freedom on the Red Riding Hood target helps them kite.

OPERA: ROMULO AND JULIANNE

Overview

Three-phase fight. Julianne first, then Romulo, then both. They must die within 10 seconds of each other or the survivor resurrects the other.

Phase 1: Julianne (alone)

  • Devotion — Self-buff increasing damage and attack speed by 50%. Purge or dispel!
  • Powerful Attraction — Holy. Stuns a random player for 6 seconds.
  • Blinding Passion — Holy. 1,500 holy damage + 3,000 over 4 seconds + disorient on a random player.
  • Eternal Affection — Holy. Large heal on herself. Must be interrupted!
  • When "killed," she feigns death and Romulo spawns.

Phase 2: Romulo (alone)

  • Backward Lunge — Physical. Knocks back the tank.
  • Daring — Self-buff increasing damage. Can be dispelled/purged.
  • Deadly Swathe — Physical. Frontal cleave dealing heavy damage. Face away from raid.
  • Poisoned Thrust — Nature. Applies a stacking poison DoT.
  • When "killed," Julianne resurrects and Phase 3 begins.

Phase 3: Both Active

  • Both retain all abilities from their solo phases.
  • Must die within 10 seconds of each other or the living one resurrects the dead one at full HP.
  • Julianne heals both — interrupts are critical.
You tank Romulo, face him away. Have a DPS or healer assigned to interrupt Julianne's heals (you can't, no interrupt). In Phase 3, OT or kite Julianne while you tank Romulo. DPS both evenly, then burn simultaneously. Romulo's Backward Lunge can punt you off the stage — tank with your back to a wall.

OPERA: THE WIZARD OF OZ

Overview

Four adds spawn first (Dorothee, Tito, Roar, Tinhead, Strawman). After all are killed, The Crone spawns. This is a Prot Paladin dream fight.

The Adds

5. The Curator

Broken Stair & Menagerie Trash

After Opera, head backstage and up. The Broken Stair has arcane creatures, and the Menagerie has more.

Broken Stair Mobs

Ghastly Haunt

  • Ethereal Curse — Reduces chance to hit with physical attacks by 15%. STACKS. Decurse frequently.
  • Shadow Shock — Shadow. Instant shadow damage
  • Undead. Can be shackled. Stacking hit reduction is very dangerous for tanks and melee.

Trapped Soul

  • Cone of Cold — Frost. Frontal AoE frost damage + slow. Face away from raid.
  • Elemental Armor — Self-buff: +200 magic resistance. Can partially resist your Holy damage. Dispel if possible.
  • Undead. Can be shackled.

Menagerie Mobs

Mana Warp (DEATH EXPLOSION)

  • Warp Breach — Arcane. ~5,500 AoE arcane explosion at low HP! Use Hammer of Justice to stun at low HP to prevent this, or have all melee run out.
  • Do NOT cleave multiple Mana Warps. Kill one at a time. Chain-exploding multiple Warps can wipe the raid.

Arcane Watchman

  • Overload — Places a lightning shield on a player causing them to periodically explode with Arcane Explosion, damaging nearby raid members. Affected player must run away from the group!
  • Hits hard in melee. Some reports say immune to taunt/stun.

Syphoner

  • Drain Mana — Arcane. Channeled mana theft on random raid members. Devastates your healers.
  • Kill quickly before healers are drained dry.
Mana Warp death explosions are the #1 trash killer in this area. Use Hammer of Justice to stun them at low HP. Never cleave multiple Warps simultaneously.

THE CURATOR

Overview

The Curator spawns Astral Flares (arcane adds) while fighting. When he runs out of mana, he enters Evocation and takes 300% increased damage. This is a DPS check and a threat fight.

Abilities

  • Hateful Bolt — Arcane. Hits the person with the second-highest threat for ~4,000-5,500 arcane damage. Fires every ~10 seconds. A designated soaker (plate DPS or OT) must stay #2 on threat.
  • Astral Flare — Arcane. Summons a small Astral Flare add every 10 seconds. Each Flare has ~17,000 HP and does ~675-825 arcane damage per hit. Must be killed quickly.
  • Evocation — At 0 mana, Curator channels Evocation for 20 seconds, taking 300% increased damage and not attacking. This is the burn phase! All DPS and cooldowns go here.
  • After Evocation, he returns to full mana and the cycle repeats.
  • Berserk — 12-minute enrage timer.
Straightforward for you. Tank Curator in place, maintain #1 threat so Hateful Bolt hits the designated #2 soaker (usually an OT or plate DPS). Assign ranged DPS to kill Flares quickly. During Evocation, pop Avenging Wrath and burn. Your biggest job is just keeping solid threat so Hateful Bolt doesn't hit a squishy.
Hateful Bolt soaker: Make sure someone in plate is consistently #2 on threat. If a healer accidentally takes #2, Hateful Bolt will destroy them.

6. Shade of Aran

Guardian's Library Trash

The library area contains arcane creatures and spell-shade mobs.

Mana Feeder (SPELL IMMUNE)

  • Astral Bite — Burns mana from anyone casting spells nearby
  • Complete immunity to ALL magical damage — Your Consecration, Holy Shield procs, Judgment, and Exorcism do ZERO damage.
  • Physical DPS only. Casters should not cast near these. Let a Warrior/Druid/Feral OT handle these.

Chaotic Sentience (SPELL IMMUNE)

  • Unstable Magic — Debuff: +300 spell damage, but +600 spell damage taken. Dispel immediately.
  • Immune to spell damage — Same as Mana Feeder. Physical DPS only.

Arcane Protector

  • Fist of Stone — Stun + undispellable debuff reducing movement speed, damage, and attack speed
  • One of the hardest-hitting mobs in the entire instance. Both tanks should maintain threat.

Spell Shade / Sorcerous Shade

  • Frostbolt / Shadow Bolt / Fireball — Various school caster nukes
  • Phasing Invisibility — Turns invisible periodically, drops target
  • Sorcerous Shade variant casts Volleys (AoE versions) — much more dangerous
  • Undead. Can be shackled. Pull with Avenger's Shield to silence. LoS pull to stack.

Arcane Anomaly

  • Arcane Volley — Arcane. Pulsing AoE arcane damage
  • Blink — Teleports to a random location, resetting threat
  • Mana Shield — Absorbs damage using its mana pool
  • Loose Mana — On death, restores mana to nearby players (kill first for healer mana!)
  • Consecrate helps re-establish threat after Blink.

Homunculus / Shadow Pillager

  • Homunculus: Firebolt — Fire. Small fire-casting demons. Come in packs. Demon — Banish available.
  • Shadow Pillager: Curse of Agony, Searing Pain, Immolate — Demon caster. Banish or interrupt.

Ethereal Thief / Ethereal Spellfilcher

  • Thief: Cleave (frontal), Disarm (8-sec), Spatial Distortion (stacking Str/Agi debuff)
  • Spellfilcher: Arcane Volley, Spatial Distortion (stacking Int/Spirit debuff — destroys healer effectiveness)
  • Humanoid — can be Polymorphed and Sapped. Pull 1-2 at a time to prevent debuff stacking.
This is the hardest trash area for Prot Paladin. Mana Feeders and Chaotic Sentiences are completely immune to spell damage — your entire kit does nothing to them. You MUST have a Warrior/Druid OT or heavy physical DPS for these. For everything else, LoS pull casters around corners. Use Hammer of Justice to stun Mana Warps at low HP to prevent their death explosion (~5,500 AoE). CC types by creature: Shackle (Undead Shades), Polymorph (Humanoid Ethereals), Banish (Demon Homunculus/Pillagers).

SHADE OF ARAN

Overview

NO TANK NEEDED. Shade of Aran is untauntable and has no aggro table — he randomly targets spells. You are DPS for this fight. The door locks when combat starts.

Abilities

  • Frostbolt — Frost. Single-target nuke. Interrupt this.
  • Fireball — Fire. Single-target nuke. Interrupt this.
  • Arcane Missiles — Arcane. Channels at a random player. Interrupt this.
  • Chains of Ice — Frost. Roots everyone in place and pulls them to the center, then casts Frost Nova (AoE ice explosion). Run to the edges of the room immediately when pulled in!
  • Flame Wreath — Fire. Places fire rings around 3 random players. DO NOT MOVE! If anyone moves through a ring, it explodes for massive raid-wide fire damage, likely wiping the group. Lasts ~20 seconds.
  • Arcane Explosion — Arcane. Aran slowly casts a massive AoE. Begins at ~40% mana. Run to the walls to outrange it. Counterspell/interrupt does NOT work on this — just run.
  • Blizzard — Frost. Rotating Blizzard around the room. Avoid standing in it. Slow + damage.
  • Mass Polymorph — Arcane. Polymorphs the entire raid and Aran drinks to restore mana. Breaks when he starts casting again. Do NOT damage him below 40% during this or he won't drink and will Pyroblast.
  • Summon Water Elementals — At 40% HP, summons 4 Water Elementals. They cast Frost bolts. AoE them down or CC (Banish/Fear).
  • Pyroblast — Fire. If anyone drinks a mana potion during the fight, Aran casts Pyroblast for ~7,000 fire damage on the whole raid. DO NOT USE MANA POTIONS in this fight (some versions of this mechanic are disputed in TBC Classic, but be safe).
You're DPS here. Swap to Seal of Righteousness and melee. Your biggest contribution: use Holy Wrath on the Water Elementals (they're Elementals, not Undead, so HW doesn't work — instead help AoE them with Consecrate). Use Blessing of Freedom on players rooted by Frost Nova after Chains of Ice pull-in. Stay calm during Flame Wreath — literally do nothing, don't even auto-attack if it might move you.
FLAME WREATH: DO. NOT. MOVE. This is the #1 wipe mechanic. If you have a fire ring around you, stand absolutely still until it fades. No strafing, no jumping, no turning with keyboard movement. This applies to everyone.

7. Terestian Illhoof

TERESTIAN ILLHOOF

Overview

Demon boss in a side room off the library hallway. He has a permanent imp companion (Kil'rek) and summons waves of Imps. Periodically sacrifices a raid member with Demon Chains. A Prot Paladin dream fight due to the Imp swarms.

Abilities

  • Shadow Bolt — Shadow. Casts at random targets for ~4,000 shadow damage.
  • Sacrifice (Demon Chains) — Shadow. Chains a random raid member to the Demon Chains portal, dealing 1,500 shadow damage per second and healing Illhoof for ~3,000/sec. The Chains have ~13,000 HP and must be DPS'd down immediately to free the player or they die.
  • Summon Imp — Summons waves of Fiendish Imps throughout the fight. They cast Firebolt at random targets. Left unchecked, they overwhelm healers.
  • Amplify Flames — Fire. Debuff on a random player increasing fire damage taken by 500. Dispel immediately or Imp Firebolts (~200 base, up to ~700 with Amplify) will destroy them.

Kil'rek (Permanent Imp Companion)

  • Broken Pact — When Kil'rek dies, Illhoof takes increased damage for ~45 seconds, then Kil'rek respawns.
  • OT or DPS handles Kil'rek. Kill him during burn phases for the damage bonus on Illhoof.
You are the Imp killer. Consecrate + Holy Shield + Retribution Aura = Imps melt. Stand near the Imp portal and keep Consecration down permanently. Your AoE threat makes this fight trivial for your raid. Tank Illhoof, keep Consecrate running for Imps, and call for DPS to switch to Demon Chains when someone gets sacrificed. This is one of your absolute best fights in all of Karazhan.
Demon Chains are the kill-or-die mechanic. Everyone must swap to Chains immediately when someone is Sacrificed. The chained player dies in about 8 seconds without intervention.

8. Netherspite

Netherspite Trash

A few packs of Nether creatures guard the path to Netherspite's room.

Nether Wraith

  • Arcane Missiles — Arcane. Channels at target
  • Blink — Teleports randomly, drops threat
  • Ghostly caster. Annoying Blink threat drops — Consecrate helps.

Netherspite Infernal

  • Hellfire — Fire. Pulsing AoE fire damage around itself
  • War Stomp — AoE stun
  • Big demon. Hits hard. Spread out to avoid War Stomp hitting everyone.

NETHERSPITE

Overview

Netherspite alternates between Portal Phase (beams active) and Banish Phase (beams gone, AoE damage). During Portal Phase, 3 colored beams connect portals on the walls to Netherspite. Players must intercept these beams.

The Three Beams (Portal Phase)

BeamColorEffect on PlayerEffect on NetherspiteWho Soaks
Perseverance Red Reduces damage taken, increases max HP, reduces healing received. Stacks — too many stacks = unhealable If it reaches him: heals him massively TANK (rotate 2 tanks)
Serenity Green Restores HP and mana rapidly. Stacking debuff reduces max HP over time If it reaches him: heals him Healer (rotate)
Dominance Blue Increases spell damage and healing. Stacking debuff increases spell damage taken If it reaches him: increases his damage DPS caster (rotate)

Portal Phase (~60 seconds)

  • 3 beams are active. Players must block all 3 from reaching Netherspite.
  • Void Zone — Shadow. Places void zones on the ground. Move out immediately! ~1,000 shadow damage every 2 seconds.
  • Each beam gives stacking buffs/debuffs. Players must rotate in and out to avoid too many stacks (especially Red — too many stacks makes you unhealable).

Banish Phase (~30 seconds)

  • Netherspite banishes himself, becoming untargetable. All beams disappear.
  • Nether Burn — Shadow. Pulsing AoE shadow damage to the entire raid (~150 per 5 sec, increasing over time). Increases in intensity. Heal through it.
  • All beam debuffs reset during this phase.
  • After Banish Phase, Portal Phase resumes with beams in new positions.

Other Abilities

  • Nether Breath — Shadow. Frontal cone dealing ~4,500 arcane damage to anyone in front of him (not tanking through the Red beam). Only the Red beam tank should be in front.
  • Enrage — 9-minute hard enrage.
Beam management is everything. As MT, you soak the Red beam. Stand between the red portal and Netherspite. After 4-5 stacks (~25 seconds), swap out with the OT (who stands behind you to take over the beam). During Banish Phase your stacks reset. Rotate: you take Red in odd portal phases, OT in even (or vice versa). Keep Holy Shield up — you'll still be getting melee'd. During Banish phase, just heal up and get ready for next beam positions.
Never let Red beam reach Netherspite — it heals him for a massive amount. If the tank dies or mispositions, someone must immediately step into the beam. Also, don't stand in front of Netherspite if you're not the Red beam tank — Nether Breath will delete you.

9. Chess Event

CHESS EVENT

Overview

A giant chess board. Each player controls one chess piece. Free loot if you don't throw. Medivh occasionally "cheats" by buffing enemy pieces or damaging yours.

Key Pieces

  • King Llane (Alliance) / Warchief Blackhand (Horde) — Your king. If he dies, you lose. Move him out of fire. Tank should control the King.
  • Conjurer/Warlock — Ranged DPS piece. Channels Rain of Fire / Fireball.
  • Charger/Wolf — Cavalry. High burst damage. Good for flanking their King.
  • Cleric/Necrolyte — Healer piece. Keeps your King and frontline alive.
  • Footman/Grunt — Pawns. Meat shields.

Medivh's Cheats

  • Medivh's Cheat: Hand of Medivh — Fire. AoE fire damage on a section of the board. Move your piece out!
  • Medivh's Cheat: Flame Shield — Buffs an enemy piece with a fire shield, dealing damage to adjacent pieces. Kill it or move away.
  • Medivh's Cheat: Fury of Medivh — Massive fire damage in an area. Move immediately.
Control the King piece. Move out of fire/cheat effects. Use your King's special abilities on cooldown. Keep the King alive and push enemy pieces. This fight is essentially a freebie — hard to fail unless people AFK. After winning, loot the chest and head up the stairs to Prince.

10. Prince Malchezaar

Prince's Tower Trash

A gauntlet of fel creatures on the spiral staircase to Prince's platform.

Greater Fleshbeast (EXTREME DANGER)

  • Thrash — Multiple extra melee attacks in quick succession (like Windfury)
  • Gaping Maw — Physical bleed DoT
  • Infectious Poison — Nature poison. Removable with Cleanse.
  • ~120,000 HP. Hits for ~3,000 melee + Thrash gives multiple hits. "Possible to die in nearly one attack." Pull ONE at a time. Use ALL defensive cooldowns. Maximum healing. Among the most dangerous trash in the entire instance.

Fleshbeast

  • Same abilities as Greater Fleshbeast (Thrash, Gaping Maw, Infectious Poison) but less HP and damage
  • Still dangerous due to Thrash. Cleanse the poison. Pull carefully.
Greater Fleshbeasts are THE most dangerous trash for Prot Paladin. Thrash can spike you from full to dead. Use Ardent Defender, Holy Shield, Ironshield Potion, and every cooldown available. Self-Cleanse the Infectious Poison between GCDs. Pull these one at a time — NEVER pull two.

PRINCE MALCHEZAAR

Overview

Three-phase fight at the top of the tower. Infernals rain from the sky periodically. Enfeeble is the key mechanic. This is a gear check and a positioning fight.

All Phases

  • Enfeeble — Shadow. Reduces 5 random raid members' HP to 1 HP for 7 seconds. Does NOT affect the highest-threat target (the tank). After Enfeeble ends, players' HP is restored. Enfeebled players must NOT take any damage or they die.
  • Shadow Word: Pain — Shadow. Applies SW:P to a random player. Standard DoT. If an Enfeebled player has SW:P, they will die. Dispel SW:P before Enfeeble hits.
  • Summon Infernal — Fire. Infernals crash down from the sky in random locations every 40 seconds (faster in later phases). They have a Hellfire aura dealing constant fire damage to anyone near them. They don't move and cannot be tanked. Bad Infernal placement can make the fight impossible.

Phase 1 (100% - 60%)

  • Standard melee attacks + Enfeeble + Shadow Word: Pain + Infernals.
  • Moderate melee damage. Tank and spank with positioning awareness.

Phase 2 (60% - 30%)

  • Sunder Armor — Stacking armor reduction (-1,000 per stack) on the tank. Stacks up to 5. Massive increase in physical damage taken.
  • Thrash — Grants 2 extra melee attacks (like Windfury).
  • Equips two axes. Hits much harder. Combined with Sunder, the tank takes enormous damage.
  • Infernals drop every 40 seconds.

Phase 3 (30% - 0%)

  • Shadow Nova — Shadow. AoE shadow damage (~3,000) to everyone within 30 yards. Frequently cast. Ranged and healers must stay at max range.
  • Axes disappear, returns to mostly spell damage. Still does Shadow Nova + Enfeeble.
  • Infernals drop every 18 seconds — space gets tight fast.
  • Damage on the tank is less than Phase 2, but raid-wide damage is very high.
Positioning is everything. Tank Prince at the edge of his platform so Infernals have maximum room. Adjust your position if an Infernal lands near you. Phase 2 is the hardest for you — Sunder Armor strips your mitigation. Use Ironshield Potion, all defensive cooldowns, and call for emergency heals during Thrash + Sunder stacks. In Phase 3, the physical damage is lighter but you need to survive Shadow Nova — Shadow Resistance gear can help if your raid has it.
Enfeeble + SW:P = instant death. Dispellers must remove SW:P from players before Enfeeble hits. Enfeebled players must immediately move away from Infernals. If an Infernal ticks on an Enfeebled player, they die at 1 HP.
Infernal management: Start Prince on one edge of the platform. If Infernals fill that side, slowly kite Prince to the other side. If extremely unlucky with Infernal placement, you may need to reset.

11. Nightbane (Summoned Boss)

NIGHTBANE

Overview

Optional boss summoned on the Master's Terrace (accessed through Opera backstage). Requires the Blackened Urn from a quest chain. Two alternating phases: Ground and Air. Undead dragon.

Ground Phase

  • Bellowing Roar — AoE fear on the entire raid. 4-second fear. ~30 second cooldown. Fear Ward, Tremor Totem, or Berserker Rage (warriors) to counter.
  • Charred Earth — Fire. Places a fire patch under a random player that deals heavy fire damage. Move out immediately.
  • Cleave — Physical. Frontal cleave. Face away from raid.
  • Distracting Ash — Fire. Reduces chance to hit by 30% on the tank. Essentially a debuff that weakens your threat.
  • Smoldering Breath — Fire. Frontal cone breath dealing 5,000-6,000 fire damage + fire DoT. Only the tank should be in front.
  • Tail Sweep — Knocks back anyone behind the dragon. Don't stand behind him.

Air Phase (at 75%, 50%, 25% HP)

  • Nightbane flies into the air and becomes untargetable for ~45-60 seconds.
  • Rain of Bones — Targets a random player. Deals physical damage in 6-yard radius and spawns 5 Restless Skeletons (~13,500 HP each, hit ~1,300 on cloth, ~450 on plate). All skeletons must die before Nightbane lands.
  • Smoking Blast — Targets the highest-threat player (tank) every ~1 second for ~15 seconds. Deals 1,850-2,150 physical damage (mitigated by armor, ~750 on plate) + 3,000 fire damage DoT over 18 seconds (dispellable). The tank must be healed through this.
  • Fireball Barrage — Fire. Triggers if anyone is more than 40 yards away. Massive raid damage. STAY WITHIN 40 YARDS of Nightbane at all times.
  • Restless Skeletons have Immolation Aura — ~1,000 fire damage/tick to nearby enemies.
Great fight for Prot Paladin. Ground phase: standard dragon tanking. Face Nightbane away, keep your back to a wall to avoid fear running you off the terrace. Fear is your biggest problem — coordinate Fear Ward from a Priest and/or Tremor Totem from a Shaman. Without fear mitigation, you'll lose threat and positioning.

Air phase: You shine! Consecrate + Holy Wrath on the Restless Skeletons (undead!). AoE tank all the skeleton adds while eating Smoking Blast damage. Make sure healers are ready for Smoking Blast — it hits hard and you're also tanking adds. Exorcism the skeletons for extra threat. When Nightbane lands, re-establish threat immediately.
Fear management is critical. Without Fear Ward or Tremor Totem, Bellowing Roar will cause the tank to run around feared while Nightbane cleaves/breathes the raid. Have fear mitigation for every fear cooldown.

12. Animal Bosses (Optional - Servants' Quarters)

One of three random animal bosses spawns in the Servants' Quarters (accessible from the entry area). They drop badges and modest loot. Which boss spawns depends on which trash mob types are present in the area.

HYAKISS THE LURKER (Spider)

Spawns when Spiders are present in Servants' Quarters.

  • Hyakiss' Web — Stuns the highest-threat target for 10 seconds! Dispellable (Magic). Boss switches to #2 threat.
  • Acidic Fang — Nature. Poison. 500 nature damage every 2 sec for 30 sec. Reduces armor by 1,000 per stack, up to 7 stacks (7,000 armor shred!). Cleansable.
  • Sneak — Starts stealthed; slows movement by 50%
Best animal boss for Prot Pally. Two tanks should build threat simultaneously — OT picks up when MT gets webbed (10 sec stun!). Self-Cleanse the Acidic Fang poison between GCDs to prevent armor shred stacks — this is a huge Paladin advantage here.

ROKAD THE RAVAGER (Darkhound)

Spawns when Darkhounds are present in Servants' Quarters.

  • Ravage — Physical. Heavy melee attack on the tank
  • Frenzied Charge — Charges a random player, briefly stunning and dealing damage
  • Enrage — At low HP, gains increased attack speed and damage
Straightforward. Tank and spank. Save cooldowns for the Enrage phase below 30%. He hits hard but has no special mechanics.

SHADIKITH THE GLIDER (Bat)

Spawns when Bats are present in Servants' Quarters.

  • Sonic Burst — AoE dealing ~2,500 damage to nearby enemies + 5-second silence. Prevents ALL spellcasting.
  • Wing Buffet — Frontal cone: 1,050-1,350 damage + knockback + threat reduction
  • Dive — Charges the farthest raid member in LoS. ~5,000 on cloth + fear on nearby targets at landing
Hardest animal boss for Prot Paladin. Sonic Burst 5-sec silence locks out your ENTIRE toolkit — no Consecrate, Holy Shield, Judgment, or any cast. Wing Buffet also reduces threat and knocks you back. Tank with back to a wall. OT must be ready to taunt after Wing Buffet. Split raid: melee/tank at one wall, ranged/healers at opposite wall. Assign someone at max range to soak Dive charges.

Servants' Quarters Trash

Coldmist Widow (Spider)

  • Poison Bolt — Nature. Ranged poison nuke
  • Frost Mist — Frost. AoE frost slow + damage
  • Can be crowd controlled. Moderate threat.

Coldmist Stalker (Spider)

  • Web — Stuns and roots a target for several seconds
  • Poison — Nature DoT
  • Stealthed. Patrols. Can surprise you. Watch for patrols.

Shadowbat / Greater Shadowbat

  • Dive — Charges a random player, dealing damage
  • Wing Buffet — Frontal cone knockback
  • Flying mobs. Come in packs. AoE them down.

Vampiric Shadowbat

  • Vampiric Touch — Shadow. Leeches HP from targets
  • Kill quickly to prevent healing.

Phase Hound / Darkhound

  • Ravage — Heavy melee strike
  • Charge — Charges a distant player
  • Simple melee mobs. Come in packs. AoE tank them.
The Servants' Quarters trash is great for AoE. Bats and spiders come in large packs — Consecrate and Holy Shield them all. Watch for spider webs and bat silences. The hounds are simple melee mobs.

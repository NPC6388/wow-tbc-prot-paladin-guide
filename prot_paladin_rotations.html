Buff Contribution Breakdown: Superior Wizard Oil & Windfury Totem

The 709 SP baseline used throughout this guide assumes Flask of Supreme Power but not Wizard Oil. This section breaks down what two situational buffs would add on top of that baseline: Superior Wizard Oil (+42 spell damage, 1 hour, self-applied to weapon) and Windfury Totem (Enhancement shaman party buff, proc-based extra melee attack).

Wizard Oil Steady-State TPS Contribution (SoR + Consec + HS rotation, no Demon/Undead):
  • SoR procs: 17.6 threat × 0.556 procs/s = +9.8 TPS
  • JoR on 10s CD: 35.9 / 10 = +3.6 TPS
  • Consec on 8s cycle: 26.8 / 8 = +3.4 TPS
  • Holy Shield on 10s cycle (full 8 charges): 38.3 / 10 = +3.8 TPS
  • Total added threat from Wizard Oil: ~21 TPS

With a 5-stack SoC + SoR swap rotation you also gain ~5 TPS from the DoT scaling (14.9 threat/tick ÷ 3s = ~5.0 TPS), bringing Wizard Oil's total to ~26 TPS on SoC fights. On Demon/Undead bosses using Exorcism + Holy Wrath, add another ~5–7 TPS from those abilities scaling. Over a 5 minute fight, Wizard Oil adds ~6,300–7,800 total threat — roughly equivalent to 4 extra JoC casts.

Wizard Oil is effectively useless if you run Seal of Blood as your primary seal — SoB procs scale only with weapon damage (0 SP coefficient), so the only scaling comes from JoB, Consec, and HS. You'd drop from ~21 TPS gain to ~11 TPS. Superior Mana Oil (+25 healing / +14 MP5) might actually be a better choice for sustained SoB tanking. But with the 709 SP / Fang of the Leviathan spell-power profile used here, SoR is the default seal — if you also choose to apply Wizard Oil, it's a clear gain on top.

Windfury Totem: Extra Melee Attacks with Bonus AP

Windfury Totem (Rank 5) passively gives the party a 20% chance on melee hit to trigger an extra attack with +445 bonus Attack Power. With a 2/2 Improved Windfury Totem talent (nearly universal on Enhancement shamans), the bonus is increased by 30% to +578 AP on the extra swing. The extra attack cannot chain-proc another Windfury (unlike Windfury Weapon, the totem has no ICD — the natural limiter is that it only procs on auto-attacks and the extra swing cannot trigger itself).

Proc Frequency (1.8s speed weapon): Swings per second = 1 / 1.8 = 0.556 Proc chance = 20% Procs per second = 0.556 × 0.20 = 0.111 Average interval = 1 / 0.111 = ~9 seconds per WF proc (No ICD — extra attack cannot chain-proc WF)

Damage of the Extra WF Attack

WF extra attack damage (1.8s weapon, 436 base AP, +578 WF bonus AP): Weapon damage roll = 41.1 DPS × 1.8s = 74.0 Normal AP bonus = 436 / 14 × 1.8 = 56.1 WF bonus AP damage = 578 / 14 × 1.8 = 74.3 Raw extra attack = 74.0 + 56.1 + 74.3 = ~204.4 physical × 1.095 crit avg (9.5% crit, 2×) = ~224 × 0.58 boss armor (raid boss ~7,700 armor) = ~130 final damage × 1.9 RF = ~247 threat per WF proc ÷ 9s interval = ~27.4 TPS from the physical extra attack

Bonus: The WF Extra Swing Also Procs Your Active Seal

The WF extra attack is treated as a normal melee swing and will trigger your active seal's on-hit effect. This is where WFT gets much more valuable for a Prot Paladin.

Assumptions and caveats:
  • Numbers include melee miss (6%) and spell miss (11.89%) from gear hit rates. WF extra attacks are subject to the same melee miss rate. Dodge/parry/glancing blow reductions are not modeled.
  • SoC stacking is chance-based (~70% per melee swing, not guaranteed). Observed average: ~13 seconds to 5 stacks with a 1.8s weapon. Simulations use expected-value fractional stacking rather than assuming 1 stack per swing.
  • Boss armor assumed at ~7,700 (raid boss baseline). A Sunder Armor stack debuff from a DPS warrior would push this higher; with 5 stacks of Sunder, physical portion gains ~10–15%.
  • WF Totem has no ICD (unlike Windfury Weapon which has a 3s hidden cooldown). The totem only procs on auto-attacks and the extra swing cannot chain-proc another WF.
  • The Imp WFT talent is nearly universal on PvE-specced Enhancement shamans. If your shaman is Elemental or Restoration and dropping WFT as a buff bot, confirm they have Imp WFT — without it, drop WFT bonus AP from 578 to 445 and all TPS gains shrink by ~10%.

Real Log Analysis: Wizard Oil vs Windfury Totem Extra Damage

This chart uses actual combat log data from a Prince Malchezaar kill (Karazhan, ~198s fight) to estimate how much additional damage each buff would contribute on top of the baseline. The log recorded 86.3k total damage with SoR active. We apply spell coefficients to every landed hit for Wizard Oil, and model WFT extra attacks using the observed melee hit rate.

Methodology: Wizard Oil adds +42 SP, scaled by each ability's spell coefficient and the actual number of landed hits from the log. WFT adds extra melee swings (~1 every 9s at 20% proc rate) that each deal physical damage (armor-reduced) plus trigger an SoR proc. The observed melee landing rate (77.6%) is applied to WFT swings. Both estimates are conservative — they don't double-count crit scaling on the delta.

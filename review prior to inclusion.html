Greater Blessing “spam” as a threat tool

TBC viability — looks good. The enabler holds in TBC: the 2.4.3 Righteous Fury tooltip reads “Increases the threat generated by your Holy spells by 60%” (base; → +90% / ×1.9 with 3/3 Improved RF). “Holy spells” is the whole Holy school — blessings, cleanses, heals included — so RF amplifies blessing threat in TBC just as it does in Classic. (An earlier draft of this note guessed RF was “Holy damage only” in TBC — the tooltip says otherwise; corrected.) And Greater Blessings are raid-wide per class in TBC (15 min), so players_buffed is the same-class count across the whole raid, not just your party — the raid-scaling upside applies (auras are the party-wide effect, blessings are not). The remaining gates are practical, not mechanical: confirm with a threat meter in 2.4.3 and pin the numbers before it goes live.

The mechanic (per the Classic Light's Bulwark guide). Non-damaging Holy-school spells — especially Greater Blessings — generate threat, and that threat is split evenly across every enemy you are in combat with. A single Greater Blessing cast buffs all same-class members at once, so threat scales with how many same-class players the cast hits:

threat per cast = base_threat × 1.90 (Improved Righteous Fury) × players_buffed

• base_threat for Greater Blessing of Kings = 60 (set by the spell's learned level) → 114 threat per target with Righteous Fury (60 × 1.9). Corroborated across sources: Warcraft Tavern — Paladin Blessings, the LibThreatClassic2 threat-meter addon (issue #29), and community testing.
• players_buffed = number of same-class players the one cast affects (e.g. all Warriors get Greater Blessing of Kings from one cast).

Why it's attractive (in Classic):

  • Unavoidable — no dodge/parry/miss against it.
  • Unresistable — not subject to partial or binary spell resistance.
  • Ranged — generates threat without attacking the target (the guide cites Onyxia P2 fireball phase: blessing-spam to stay high on threat while at range).
  • Global / AOE — the threat is split across all mobs in combat, so it tops up threat on a whole pack at once, and scales hugely in stacked raids (many same-class players per cast).

To do before inclusion: (1) spot-check with a TBC 2.4.3 threat meter that a GBoK cast actually scales with Righteous Fury as the tooltip implies (the 114/target value itself is well-sourced for Classic); (2) decide where it fits the GCD priority and mana budget, and frame its niche (ranged/AOE/unavoidable threat top-up — while kited, during a fireball phase, or pre-positioning); (3) if useful, pull base_threat for the other Greater Blessings. Sources: Light's Bulwark (Wowhead Classic), Warcraft Tavern, LibThreatClassic2 #29.

GBoK spam vs Consecration over one Consecration cooldown (raid scenario)

The fair comparison: Consecration is one cast per 8s cooldown, and in that same 8s you can fire ~5 Greater Blessings (8s ÷ 1.5s GCD). So compare threat per 8-second window — one Consecration (2,320, landed in full on each enemy) vs 5 GBoK casts (114 × players_buffed each, split across all enemies). Two cases: a single raid boss, and a 5-pack (shown as per-enemy threat, since that's what holds aggro).

Single-target raid boss: 5 GBoK casts overtake one Consecration's per-CD threat at just ~4 same-class players (2,320 ÷ (5 × 114) ≈ 4.1) — very achievable in a 25-man (a full melee group of one class). At 5 players it's ~2,850 vs 2,320; at 8, ~4,560. On a single-target boss, GBoK spam is genuinely competitive with — even ahead of — Consecration's throughput.

5-pack: Consecration hits all five for full (2,320 each), while GBoK's flat threat splits across them — so per enemy it's 5× weaker and the crossover jumps to ~20 players (off-chart, unrealistic). Consecration dominates AOE.

Reality check: “5 GBoK in 8s” means 5 GCDs doing nothing else — no Holy Shield (you'd drop crush immunity), no Judgement, no seal — so it's a GCD-for-GCD ceiling, not a full-rotation swap. Its real value is spare-GCD and ranged threat: on a single-target raid boss with a stacked class, or any time you're out of melee (kiting, fireball phases), weaving Greater Blessing of Kings is strong supplemental threat. It's mana-hungry, so it wants steady incoming healing.

Essential Paladin Macros

The macros that make paladin tanking practical — the mouseover taunt first, then the seal-and-judge, cooldown, and utility macros referenced throughout the rotation sections.

Righteous Defense (Taunt) — Most Important Macro

#showtooltip Righteous Defense
/cast [target=mouseover,help,nodead] Righteous Defense; [target=targettarget,help,nodead] Righteous Defense

Mouseover a raid frame to taunt enemies off that player. Falls back to your target's target. Bind this and practice it.

Righteous Defense (Focus Target Variant)

#showtooltip Righteous Defense
/cast [target=mouseover,help,nodead] Righteous Defense; [target=focus,help,nodead] Righteous Defense; Righteous Defense

Set your focus on the main tank. Mouseover takes priority, then focus target, then current target.

Divine Shield + Cancel (Emergency Immunity)

#showtooltip Divine Shield
/cast Divine Shield
/cancelaura Divine Shield

First press: activates Divine Shield. Second press: cancels it immediately. Use to survive a one-shot mechanic, then cancel to resume tanking.

Seal Swap Macro

#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift] Seal of Wisdom; [mod:ctrl] Seal of Righteousness; [mod:alt] Seal of Blood; Seal of Corruption

Default: Seal of Corruption (sustained single-target threat). Ctrl: Seal of Righteousness (raid pull opener — Judge Righteousness for snap threat, plus AOE / short fights). Shift: Seal of Wisdom (mana recovery). Alt: Seal of Blood (low-gear pull opener only, below ~400 SP — Judge Blood).

Avenging Wrath + Notification

#showtooltip Avenging Wrath
/cast Avenging Wrath
/run SendChatMessage("AVENGING WRATH - HIGH THREAT", "RAID_WARNING")

Pops wings and warns the raid. Use on pull or burn phases.

Emergency Cooldown Stack

#showtooltip Divine Protection
/cast Holy Shield
/cast Divine Protection
/use 13

Activates Holy Shield + Divine Protection + top trinket slot simultaneously. Use for predictable damage spikes.

Aura Switching

#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift] Shadow Resistance Aura; [mod:ctrl] Fire Resistance Aura; Devotion Aura

Quick aura swaps for different bosses/phases.

Engineering Pull (Rocket Launcher)

#showtooltip
/use [mod:shift] Goblin Sapper Charge; Goblin Rocket Launcher

Default: Rocket Launcher for ranged pulls. Shift: Sapper Charge for AOE threat.

How it works: Defense and Resilience both convert to crit reduction % through different rates, and the percentages simply add together. There is no single "resilience to defense" conversion — they meet in the middle at crit reduction %.

Defense Skill (B1) − 350 → Skill from Gear × 0.04 → Crit Reduction %
Resilience Rating (B2) ÷ 39.4231 → Crit Reduction %
Total = sum of both

Crush Immunity — Inputs

Crushing blows deal 150% normal damage and can only be landed by mobs 3+ levels above you (level 73 raid bosses). Heroic dungeon bosses (level 72) cannot crush. To push crushing blows off the combat table, your combined avoidance must reach 102.4%. Holy Shield adds 30% block chance while active, which is why paladins are the easiest tanks to make uncrushable.

Read these values from your character sheet (press C → Defense tab). Enter percentages without Holy Shield active — the sheet adds it automatically. Character sheet values already include contributions from Defense skill.

Block Rating is by far the cheapest way to reach uncrushable (7.88 rating per 1% vs 18.92 for dodge or 23.65 for parry). Combined with Holy Shield's 30%, this is why paladins are the only tank class that can realistically reach 102.4% in early tiers. Resilience does not help here at all — it only reduces crit chance, not the combat table.

Sanctity (0/38/23) Talent Trade-offs

Two point-shuffles for the Sanctity prot-paladin build, grounded in the spell-power coefficients and threat math from the main guide. Single-target boss, Judgement of the Crusader maintained. Part 1: Conviction vs Improved Seal of the Crusader (a near-wash). Part 2: One-Handed Weapon Specialization vs Combat Expertise (not close).

Part 1 — Conviction vs Improved Seal of the Crusader

The two configs compared:
  • Config A — 2/5 Conviction + 2/3 Improved SotC (the guide's current Sanctity build)
  • Config B — 1/5 Conviction + 3/3 Improved SotC (move 1 point from Conviction to Imp SotC)
Same gear, same rotation, same everything else — only this 1 point differs.

Why the trade is almost a wash — and which way it tips

The two talents both grant crit, but they are not the same crit:

TalentWhat it givesApplies to
Conviction (1%/rank) Melee crit only Your melee swings, against any target. (Wowhead: melee weapons only — does not touch spell crit.)
Improved SotC (1%/rank) +crit-to-be-hit on the JotC target, all attacks (melee + spell) Everyone's hits — including your spell crits — against the boss while Judgement of the Crusader is up. (Warcraft Wiki: TBC 2.3+ is purely the crit debuff; the old damage bonus was folded into the base spell, so the +219 Holy-damage-taken debuff is identical at 2/3 and 3/3.)

On a single boss with JotC up, your crit against that boss comes out as:

Crit vs the bossConfig A
(2 Conv / 2 ImpSotC)		Config B
(1 Conv / 3 ImpSotC)		Difference
Melee crit (auto-attacks, JoB)+2% (Conv) +2% (ImpSotC) = +4%+1% (Conv) +3% (ImpSotC) = +4%identical
Spell crit (Judgements, Exo, HoW)+2% (ImpSotC only)+3% (ImpSotC only)+1% for Config B

Conviction can't help your spell crit, so the point you take out of it costs you nothing on spell damage; the point you put into Imp SotC adds +1% spell crit on the boss. The melee side breaks even (both reach +4%). So the entire personal-threat difference is +1% spell crit on your crit-able Holy abilities.

What +1% spell crit is worth (guide coefficients)

Of the prot rotation, only the judgements, Exorcism, and Holy Wrath can spell-crit. Consecration, Holy Shield, the SoR proc, and the SoC DoT cannot crit, so they're unaffected. The Sanctity build has no Avenger's Shield. Each crit is ×1.5 (spell), so +1% crit = +0.5% average damage on that ability.

Verdict

The one case for Config A (more Conviction): Conviction's melee crit works on every target and even before JotC lands, while Imp SotC only helps against the one JotC-debuffed mob. On heavy multi-target / AOE pulls — where your auto-attacks and any melee procs hit several mobs that aren't JotC'd — the extra Conviction point claws back a little. It's still small (auto-attacks are a minor share of prot threat), but it's the only scenario where A is ahead. On a sustained single boss, B wins.
Guide note: the main guide currently says 2/3 Imp SotC "strengthens the Judgement of the Crusader Holy-damage debuff." That was true in vanilla but not in TBC 2.3+ — the damage component was folded into the base spell, so the +219 debuff is the same at any Imp SotC rank. The talent is purely the crit debuff. (This page assumes the correct TBC behavior; it doesn't change the verdict, but the guide line is worth fixing.)

Part 2 — One-Handed Weapon Specialization vs Combat Expertise

The Sanctity build runs 3/5 One-Handed Weapon Specialization and 3/5 Combat Expertise. Unlike Part 1, this trade is not close — the two talents do completely different things:

TalentWhat it givesThreat reach
1H Weapon Spec (2%/rank) +2% to ALL damage you deal (every Holy ability + melee) Multiplies your entire threat — Consecration, Holy Shield, judgements, seal procs, the SoC DoT, all of it.
Combat Expertise (+1 exp, +2% Sta /rank) +1 Expertise (−0.25% boss dodge & parry) and +2% Stamina Expertise only un-dodges the melee-table slice — auto-attacks and seal procs. Judgements, Consecration, Holy Shield, and the SoC DoT cannot be dodged (spells / block-triggered / an already-applied debuff), so expertise does nothing for them.

Running the guide's own reference simulation for the Sanctity build (~1,428 TPS with JotC up), one point each way is worth:

MoveThreat changeOther effects
+1 rank One-Handed Weapon Spec (e.g. 3/5 → 4/5)+27 TPS (~+1.9%)none
+1 rank Combat Expertise (e.g. 3/5 → 4/5)~+0 TPS (negligible)+2% Stamina (~250–280 HP) & −0.25% boss parry (parry-haste safety)

Why Combat Expertise is ~0 threat: almost none of a prot paladin's threat is dodgeable. The big sources — Consecration, Holy Shield, Judgement of Corruption, the SoC DoT — are spells, block-triggers, or an already-applied debuff. The only dodgeable threat is auto-attacks (physical, not amplified by Righteous Fury, so tiny) and the occasional Seal of Righteousness proc during the swap window. Shaving 0.25% off boss dodge barely moves that sliver, while One-Handed Weapon Spec lifts everything by 2%.

For threat: max One-Handed Weapon Specialization before Combat Expertise. Each 1H Weapon Spec rank is worth ~+27 TPS (~+1.9%); each Combat Expertise rank is worth essentially nothing for threat. If TPS is the only goal, move every spare Combat Expertise point into 1H Weapon Spec (toward 5/5).

The catch — Combat Expertise is a survivability talent, not a threat one. Its value is the +2% Stamina per rank and parry-haste reduction (each expertise removes 0.25% boss parry; a parried swing accelerates the boss's next hit, which can spike a tank). So keep Combat Expertise as your defensive floor on progression, and shift its points into 1H Weapon Spec on farm / threat-when-you-want-it content. Unlike Part 1, this is a threat-vs-survivability dial, not a threat-vs-threat wash.

Reference profile (both parts): 709 SP, 1.9× Righteous Fury, 88.11% spell hit vs a level-73 boss, JotC +219 Holy-damage-taken debuff maintained, Seal of Corruption at 5 stacks.

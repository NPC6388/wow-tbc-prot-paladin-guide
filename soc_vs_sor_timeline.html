When Does SoC Overtake SoR?

Seal of Corruption (SoC) applies a stacking DoT on each melee hit, up to 5 stacks. Each stack adds both DoT damage and increases the Judgement damage. The question is: at how many stacks does SoC's cumulative threat surpass SoR?

SoC + SoR Seal Swap at 5 Stacks

Once Corruption reaches 5 stacks, the DoT continues ticking regardless of which seal you have active — the stacks are a debuff on the target, not tied to your current seal. This means you can switch to Seal of Righteousness to gain SoR's per-swing procs on top of the 5-stack DoT. You get both.

Stack Maintenance: When to Swap Back

Corruption stacks last 15 seconds from the last melee hit with SoC active. If you switch to SoR, the timer keeps counting down. To guarantee stacks don't fall off, switch back to Seal of Corruption ~9 seconds before the stacks expire (i.e., after ~6 seconds on SoR). Your next melee hit with SoC active refreshes the 15-second timer.

Seal swap cycle (once at 5 stacks):
1. SoC active → melee hit refreshes stacks (15s timer starts)
2. Switch to SoR → SoR procs + SoC DoT both generating threat (~6 seconds)
3. Switch back to SoC ~9s before stacks expire → next melee refreshes stacks
4. When Judgement CD is ready: Judge Corruption (5 stacks) → re-seal SoC → melee refreshes → switch SoR
5. Repeat

Judgement of Wisdom for Mana Sustain

Long fights require mana sustain. Judgement of Wisdom (JoW) applies a debuff that restores mana to all attackers on hit — critical for the raid and your own sustain. However, JoW does zero direct damage, so you want to time it carefully to minimize threat loss.

JoW timing rules:
1. Never judge Wisdom before your opener judgement (JoB or JoC) — snap threat is critical.
2. Never judge Wisdom before your first 5-stack JoC — that's your biggest threat spike.
3. Third judge (~23s) is the earliest safe window for JoW.
4. Seal Wisdom 1 GCD before Judgement comes off CD, judge immediately, then re-seal Corruption to maintain stacks.
5. All subsequent judges after JoW go back to Judgement of Corruption (5-stack).
JoW seal-twist sequence: Seal of Wisdom (1 GCD) → Judgement of Wisdom → Seal of Corruption (re-seal). Costs 3 GCDs total and one 5-stack JoC worth of threat, but the Wisdom debuff lasts the rest of the fight.
Mana cost: Each seal swap costs a GCD + ~210 mana. With two swaps per judge cycle (~10s), that's ~420 extra mana per cycle and 2 GCDs. Factor this into mana management on long fights. JoW debuff helps offset this.

Totem Scaling: Windfury vs Wrath of Air on the Seal Swap

Using the seal swap rotation above, this comparison asks: which totem scales with the paladin's gear? The same rotation is run twice at each spell-damage level — once with Imp Windfury Totem (extra melee swings, +578 bonus AP), once with Wrath of Air Totem (+101 spell damage to the party).

What scales with what:
  • Windfury Totem — The extra swing's physical damage scales with the paladin's AP (≈436 here, mostly fixed by Prot gearing). Crucially, the extra swing also procs the active seal, and that proc scales with the paladin's spell damage via the seal's SP coefficient. So WFT's value does grow as the paladin gears up — more SP means stronger seal procs on every WF-extra-swing.
  • Wrath of Air Totem — +101 SP is a flat raid buff. That +101 scales every SP-coefficient ability in the rotation (SoR procs, SoC DoT ticks, JoC/JoR judgements). But the +101 itself never grows — whether the paladin starts with 400 or 800 SP, WoA still only adds 101. Its contribution is fixed.
Two charts below: at SP=400 (mid-gear) and SP=800 (heavily buffed end-game). If WoA's line tracks WFT's line proportionally, both scale equally. If WFT widens its lead as SP rises, WFT scales better with gear.

Avenging Wrath & Opener Comparison

Avenging Wrath (AW) grants +30% damage for 20 seconds on a 3-minute cooldown. It also causes Forbearance in TBC 2.4.3.

AW is instant cast (on the 1.5s GCD) and can be used mid-combat. This means you have a choice: pop it pre-pull to boost your opener (Avenger's Shield, first Judgement, early Consecrations), or delay it to 5 stacks (~9s in) so the +30% multiplies your peak DoT and full-power Judgement of Corruption. Both approaches are shown in the rotations below. Causes Forbearance.

Third Opener: Judgement of the Crusader (Debuff Opener)

A third opener option worth considering when a co-tank/DPS handles initial aggro: open with Seal of the Crusader → Judgement of the Crusader to apply the +219 Holy damage taken debuff permanently. Auto-attacks refresh the debuff, so it only needs to be judged once. JotC itself does trivial direct threat (~71), but the debuff amplifies every subsequent Holy ability for the entire fight.

JotC Debuff Amplification (permanent, per ability):
  • Consecration: +219 per tick × 7 ticks × 1.9 RF = +2,913 extra threat per cast. Every Consec for the entire fight.
  • Holy Shield blocks: +219 each × 3 blocks × 1.9 = +1,248 extra threat per HS cast. Every HS for the entire fight.
  • Judgements (JoC/JoB/JoR): +219 × 1.9 = +416 extra threat per judge. Every judge for the entire fight.
  • SoC DoT ticks: The debuff adds to each Holy hit's base damage, not the DoT per-tick calculation (DoT uses snapshot math). Treat this as a minor bonus.
  • Exorcism / Holy Wrath (if Undead/Demon): +219 × 1.9 × ~1.05 crit = +437 each.
The debuff is permanent (auto-attacks refresh it) — these gains compound for the entire fight, not just a 20s window. Cost: only 1 judge slot (the opener) + 92 threat instead of ~1,492 from JoB. Break-even in under 10 seconds.
When to use JotC opener:
  • Any fight where pull snap is covered — the debuff is permanent and amplifies everything, so it's always a net gain after ~10s.
  • Co-tank scenarios where another tank holds initial aggro (Maulgar, High King, Archimonde transitions).
  • When Misdirection / Tricks of the Trade is available from a hunter or rogue to cover the weak opener.
When NOT to use JotC opener:
  • Solo pull, no MD/ToT: JoB snap (~1,492) dominates for the first 3 seconds of threat. You need aggro RIGHT NOW.
  • Another paladin is already keeping JotC up — you'd double-apply for no benefit. Swap to JoB opener.

Windfury Totem: Extra Melee Attacks with Bonus AP

Windfury Totem (Rank 5) passively gives the party a 20% chance on melee hit to trigger an extra attack with +445 bonus Attack Power. With a 2/2 Improved Windfury Totem talent (nearly universal on Enhancement shamans), the bonus is increased by 30% to +578 AP on the extra swing. The extra attack cannot chain-proc another Windfury (unlike Windfury Weapon, the totem has no ICD — the natural limiter is that it only procs on auto-attacks and the extra swing cannot trigger itself).

Proc Frequency (1.8s speed weapon): Swings per second = 1 / 1.8 = 0.556 Proc chance = 20% Procs per second = 0.556 × 0.20 = 0.111 Average interval = 1 / 0.111 = ~9 seconds per WF proc (No ICD — extra attack cannot chain-proc WF)

Damage of the Extra WF Attack

WF extra attack damage (1.8s weapon, 436 base AP, +578 WF bonus AP): Weapon damage roll = 41.1 DPS × 1.8s = 74.0 Normal AP bonus = 436 / 14 × 1.8 = 56.1 WF bonus AP damage = 578 / 14 × 1.8 = 74.3 Raw extra attack = 74.0 + 56.1 + 74.3 = ~204.4 physical × 1.095 crit avg (9.5% crit, 2×) = ~224 × 0.58 boss armor (raid boss ~7,700 armor) = ~130 final damage × 1.9 RF = ~247 threat per WF proc ÷ 9s interval = ~27.4 TPS from the physical extra attack

Bonus: The WF Extra Swing Also Procs Your Active Seal

The WF extra attack is treated as a normal melee swing and will trigger your active seal's on-hit effect. This is where WFT gets much more valuable for a Prot Paladin.

Assumptions and caveats:
  • Numbers include melee miss (6%) and spell miss (11.89%) from gear hit rates. WF extra attacks are subject to the same melee miss rate. Dodge/parry/glancing blow reductions are not modeled.
  • SoC stacking is chance-based (~70% per melee swing, not guaranteed). Observed average: ~13 seconds to 5 stacks with a 1.8s weapon. Simulations use expected-value fractional stacking rather than assuming 1 stack per swing.

Fight-Length Rotation Guide: Max Threat by Duration

These rotations maximize total threat over the fight duration using the hybrid opener (3A snap + 3B JotC). JotC is judged once at J2 — auto-attacks refresh the debuff permanently, so every ability for the rest of the fight is amplified by +219 Holy damage. The only variable between fight lengths is when (and whether) to sacrifice a judge slot to JoW for mana. Shorter fights skip JoW entirely and rely on a Super Mana Potion; longer fights use JoW at the minimum frequency needed to sustain mana, placed to avoid wasting AW-boosted judge slots.

Universal rules (all fight lengths):
  1. Holy Shield: 100% uptime. Cast on CD every 10s, no exceptions. This is your #1 GCD priority.
  2. Consecration on CD (every 8s). #2 GCD priority.
  3. Exorcism on CD if demon/undead boss (e.g. Prince, Nightbane). Fits in empty GCDs between Consec/HS.
  4. Avenger's Shield on CD (every 30s). Lower priority than HS/Consec/Exo but significant per-cast threat.
  5. Seal management between judges (see below).
Seal management between judges: Judgement consumes your active seal, so you must re-seal after every judge. Your default seal is Seal of Corruption (SoC) — it builds and maintains your 5-stack DoT. Here's what happens around each judge type:
  • JoB (opener only): SoB is already active from pre-pull → Judge Blood → re-seal SoC (macro). Start building stacks.
  • JotC (once only, at J2): Seal SotC 1 GCD before judge CD → Judge Crusader → re-seal SoC. Costs 2 extra seals (SotC + SoC) = 420 mana beyond the judge itself. Auto-attacks refresh the debuff permanently — never needs re-judging. Your SoC stacks keep ticking from the DoT — you only lose ~1.5s of auto-attack stack refreshes.
  • JoC: SoC is already active → Judge Corruption → damage scales with current stacks, stacks are preserved and timer is refreshed. If at 5 stacks: re-seal SoR to ride per-swing procs while the DoT keeps ticking at full power. If below 5 stacks: re-seal SoC to keep building. Cheapest judge (just 1 re-seal = 210 mana).
  • JoW: Seal SoW 1 GCD before judge CD → Judge Wisdom → re-seal SoC. Costs 2 extra seals (SoW + SoC) = 420 mana, but the 10s JoW debuff returns ~386 mana from auto-attacks (net +176).
SoR swap at 5 stacks (~16.5s): Once SoC reaches 5 stacks, your DoT is ticking at max rate regardless of which seal is active. Swap to Seal of Righteousness for per-swing Holy procs while the DoT continues. Ride both for ~5s, then swap back to SoC before the 15s stack timer expires (stacks refresh on each SoC melee hit). SoC has ~70% chance per swing to apply a stack, averaging ~13s to reach 5 stacks with a 1.8s weapon.
Only 4 stacks at judge time? Judge immediately — do not delay.
With bad RNG, you may only have 4 SoC stacks when the 10s judge CD comes up. It is not worth waiting for the 5th stack. The math:
  • JoC at 5 stacks vs 4 stacks: +213 threat (1,483 vs 1,270 after spell hit).
  • Waiting 1 auto (1.8s) gives only a 70% chance to proc the 5th stack → expected gain: +149 threat.
  • The delay pushes every subsequent judge back by 1.8s, costing ~1,694 threat of lost output at the end of a fixed-length fight.
  • You also miss 1 SoR proc (~305 threat) from the delayed re-seal.
Net: −1,545 threat. Always judge on CD, even at 4 stacks. Re-seal SoC (not SoR) and keep building — you'll reach 5 before the next judge.
Pre-pull (all fights): −6.0s Seal of Blood → −4.5s Holy Shield → −3.0s Consecration → −1.5s Avenging Wrath → 0.0s Avenger's Shield (PULL)
Reading the judge sequences below: JoB-SoC = Judge Blood → re-seal Corruption • JotC-SoC = Judge Crusader → re-seal Corruption • JoC-SoR = Judge Corruption (5-stack) → re-seal Righteousness • JoW-SoC = Judge Wisdom → re-seal Corruption • Judges fire every 10s starting at 1.5s. Between every pair of judges: HS > Consec > AS > Exo > seal management.
JoW Mana Economy (single judgement window): When you judge Wisdom, the Wisdom debuff is active until your next judge (10s later) overwrites it — exactly one 10-second window. The JotC damage debuff is not affected — auto-attacks keep it refreshed independently.

During that 10s window, your auto-attacks (~5.2 landed hits at 1.8s swing / 94% hit) each return 74 mana from the JoW debuff — totaling ~386 mana returned. The extra cost to cast JoW (Seal of Wisdom swap) is 210 mana beyond your normal judge+re-seal cost.

Net mana per JoW window: ~+176 mana (386 returned − 210 extra seal cost). This is your personal gain only — every melee attacker in the raid also gets 74 mana per hit on the JoW-debuffed target, making it a significant raid-wide mana tool even though your personal net is modest.

Key Takeaways

  • SoC overtakes SoR in per-second output once enough stacks are applied (see crossover table above). Cumulative threat crossover takes longer because SoR had a head start during the ramp-up.
  • SoR swap at 5 stacks is free threat. The DoT keeps ticking while SoR procs add TPS on top. Switch back to SoC 9s before stacks expire to maintain them.
  • Judge Wisdom on the 3rd judge (~23s) for mana sustain. Seal SoW 1 GCD before judge CD, judge immediately, re-seal SoC. Costs one JoC but the Wisdom debuff lasts the fight.

  • Target switches kill SoC. Stick with SoR for multi-target or frequent swaps.

Real Log Analysis: Wizard Oil vs Windfury Totem Extra Damage

This chart uses actual combat log data from a Prince Malchezaar kill (Karazhan, ~198s fight) to estimate how much additional damage each buff would contribute on top of the baseline. The log recorded 86.3k total damage with SoR active. We apply spell coefficients to every landed hit for Wizard Oil, and model WFT extra attacks using the observed melee hit rate.

Methodology: Wizard Oil adds +42 SP, scaled by each ability's spell coefficient and the actual number of landed hits from the log. WFT adds extra melee swings (~1 every 9s at 20% proc rate) that each deal physical damage (armor-reduced) plus trigger an SoR proc. The observed melee landing rate (77.6%) is applied to WFT swings. Both estimates are conservative — they don't double-count crit scaling on the delta.

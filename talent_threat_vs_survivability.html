Threat vs Survivability: A Prot Paladin Talent Mini-Guide

Every talent that moves your threat or your survival, sorted, rated, and assembled into three specs — max threat, balanced, and max survivability. Built on the validated builds and threat math in the main guide. Companion to the Sanctity talent trade-off page.

The hard constraint first: in TBC, no amount of threat matters if you're dead. Every tanking spec — including the max-threat one — must keep you uncrittable (490 defense skill vs a level-73 boss, with Anticipation covering 20 of it) and uncrushable (102.4% combined avoidance + block with Holy Shield up). That fixes a shared spine of talents in all three specs below; everything else is the threat-vs-survival dial.

The non-negotiable core (all three specs keep this): Anticipation 5/5, Redoubt 5/5, Holy Shield 1/1 + Improved Holy Shield 2/2, Improved Righteous Fury 3/3, Blessing of Sanctuary 1/1, Precision 3/3, Benediction 5/5 (Ret). That's ~25 points that buy uncrittable + uncrushable + the 1.9× threat engine + mana to run it. The remaining ~36 points are where the three specs diverge.

The three specs

1 — Max Threat  0/38/23 (the guide's Sanctity build, tuned)

Take the guide's Sanctity build and apply the two trade-off findings: push 1H Weapon Spec to 5/5 and Improved Seal of the Crusader to 3/3, paid for by dropping Combat Expertise to 1/5 and Conviction to 1/5. Keeps Sanctity Aura + Improved Sanctity Aura + Crusade for the damage multipliers.

vs the balanced buildChange
One-Handed Weapon Spec3/5 → 5/5 (+4% all damage, ~+54 TPS)
Improved Seal of the Crusader2/3 → 3/3 (+1% raid-wide crit on the boss)
Combat Expertise3/5 → 1/5 (−4% Stamina, −2 expertise / parry-haste)
Conviction2/5 → 1/5 (melee crit you don't use anyway)

Push it further (glass-cannon): drop Deflection 5/5 → 2/5 for Sanctified Seals 3/3 — trades 3% parry for +3% spell crit on your judgements. Only on farm content where threat is the whole point. Verdict: highest TPS, thinnest EH; run it when healers are comfortable and you want to bury the meter.

2 — Balanced  0/43/18 (the guide's Avenger's Shield build) — calculator

The all-rounder and the default recommendation. Full Combat Expertise 5/5 and Toughness 5/5 for the survival floor, 1H Weapon Spec 4/5 + Crusade 3/3 for threat, and Avenger's Shield for a ranged pull and strong AOE. Self-sufficient (brings its own Kings); doesn't depend on a raid Ret paladin for Sanctity.

Carries both sidesDetail
SurvivalCombat Expertise 5/5, Toughness 5/5, Sacred Duty 2/2, Deflection 5/5 — full stamina/armor/parry floor
Threat1H Weapon Spec 4/5, Crusade 3/3, Avenger's Shield (pull + AOE), Improved Holy Shield 2/2

Verdict: ~90% of the max-threat spec's TPS with a real survival cushion and a ranged pull. If you don't have a reason to pick another, pick this.

3 — Max Survivability  ≈0/51/10 (confirm pathing in a calculator)

Strip the Ret threat package down to Benediction 5/5 + Deflection 5/5 and pour everything into Protection's defensive depth. The threat engine still runs (Improved Righteous Fury, Holy Shield, Blessing of Sanctuary, Precision are all core) — you just stop multiplying it.

Added vs balancedWhy
Ardent Defender 5/5−30% damage below 35% HP — the clutch survival talent
Spell Warding 2/2−4% magic damage for caster bosses
Shield Specialization 3/3+30% block value — more absorbed per block
Combat Expertise 5/5 + Toughness 5/5 + Sacred Duty 2/2full stamina + armor floor kept
DroppedSanctity Aura, Improved Sanctity Aura, Crusade, Improved SotC, most of 1H Weapon Spec — the threat multipliers

Verdict: for progression bosses that hit like a truck or a tight EH check. Your threat ceiling drops (no Sanctity/Crusade/1HWS), so lean on a Ret paladin's Sanctity Aura + JotC to backfill threat from outside your spec.

Bottom line. The threat-vs-survival dial in a prot paladin is narrow — the 1.9× Righteous Fury engine, Holy Shield, and the uncrit/uncrush core are shared, so all three specs tank and all three hold threat. You're really choosing where ~36 leftover points go:
  • Max Threat spends them on damage multipliers (Sanctity, 1H Weapon Spec 5/5, crit) and accepts thinner EH.
  • Balanced splits them — full stamina/armor floor and a ranged pull, ~90% of the threat. The default.
  • Max Survival spends them on Ardent Defender + mitigation depth and leans on the raid for outside threat help.
Most nights you want Balanced; bring Max Threat to farm and Max Survival to the wall you keep dying on.

Threat / survivability indices below are relative, talents-only estimates for the same gear: threat from the stacked damage multipliers (1H Weapon Spec, Sanctity Aura, Improved Sanctity Aura, Crusade) and crit talents; survivability from stamina %, armor %, Ardent Defender, parry/block, and Spell Warding. They illustrate the trade, not exact in-game numbers.
